How it works legally

Your rights under UK and EU law

Legal basis
UK261 Regulations 2019 (post-Brexit) and EU Regulation 261/2004 cover most delayed and cancelled flights. These rights apply to millions of passengers every year.
What we check
Route distance, delay duration, disruption type, extraordinary circumstances defence, care and assistance rights, and the 6-year limitation period.
What we are not

Questions

Everything you need to know

How much could I be owed?
£220 for flights up to 1,500 km, £350 for 1,500–3,500 km, and £520 for flights over 3,500 km. These amounts apply per passenger — so a family of four could be owed over £2,000.
Does this apply to my flight?
UK261/EU261 covers flights departing from a UK or EU airport (any airline), and flights arriving in the UK or EU operated by a UK or EU airline. If your flight was delayed 3+ hours at arrival, or cancelled without 14 days’ notice, you may have a claim.
What if the airline says “extraordinary circumstances”?
Airlines often use this defence to avoid paying. But technical faults, staff shortages and most airline strikes do not qualify as extraordinary circumstances under case law. We check whether the airline’s defence actually holds up.
How far back can I claim?
In England and Wales you have 6 years to claim. In Scotland it’s 5 years. So you may be able to claim for flights that were delayed or cancelled years ago — as long as you have some documentation.
What if the airline ignores my letter?
Your claim letter explains the next steps clearly. If the airline ignores or rejects your claim, you can escalate to the Civil Aviation Authority (CAA), an approved Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) scheme, or take the case to the Small Claims Court.
Is this legal advice?
No. FlightClaim provides an informational check and a draft claim letter. We don’t provide legal advice or represent you. For complex situations, we recommend Citizens Advice or a regulated solicitor.
What happens to my document?
Your document is permanently deleted after analysis. We do not store your personal data beyond what is needed to process your request.
